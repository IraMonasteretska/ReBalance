How Collagen Therapy Works?

Microneedling collagen therapy is using a micro-pen that contains 12 super tiny needles. It creates little perforations in the surface of the skin that encourage the production of collagen and allow better application of facial products.

Your skin accepts topical ointments or creams more readily with microneedling because the tiny holes reach beneath the skin’s surface. As your skin heals itself from the perforations, it produces extra elastin and collagen to repair the skin, resulting in thicker skin, reduced wrinkles, softer scars and a more even skin tone.

Topicals Commonly Used with Microneedling

Topical ointments and creams aren’t always used with microneedling collagen therapy, but it’s a procedure that increases the efficacy of topicals. The tiny holes the needles create allow the ointments to get deeper into the skin.

Some topicals commonly used with microneedling include:

  • Vitamin C. This is a powerful vitamin that packs a boost of antioxidants while it strengthens skin.
  • Hyaluronic serum. This is a substance that naturally occurs in your body to help your skin retain moisture. When it’s used in microneedling, it causes the skin to plump up so it appears firm. It also evens out your skin tone, leaving you with a bright youthful complexion.
  • Stem cells. This method uses stem cells in combination with microneedling collagen therapy, which stimulate collagen and elastin production. It’s used to reduce discolorations, wrinkles, acne, scars and stretch marks.
  • Lightening serums. There are a number of serums available that allow your aesthetic specialist to lighten or even out your skin tone through concentrated applications combined with microneedling collagen therapy.

Platelet Rich Plasma Therapy and Microneedling in Our Center

PRP therapy and microneedling collagen therapy are a safe and natural treatment that rejuvenate and revitalize your skin so you look as young as you feel. This is a completely natural process that allows your skin to rejuvenate itself with the help of your aesthetic specialist. Microneedling with PRP treatment uses the platelets from your own body. Your blood is collected and used to make a concentrated serum, which is why the microneedling PRP procedure also is referred to as a Platelet Rich Plasma (PRP) facial.

This serum contains three to five times more platelets than found in the blood. Platelets naturally promote healing processes in your skin and when injected in this concentrated serum, it activates your skin’s healing process and leaves your face looking rejuvenated and fresh.

PRP is a therapy that’s been used in neurosurgery, orthopedic surgery and cosmetic surgery for more than 20 years. There is little risk because the material being injected into your skin is from your own body and the needles are very small and cause minimal skin trauma. PRP therapy and microneedling are a safe and natural treatment that rejuvenate and revitalize your skin so you look as young as you feel.

What to Expect During Your Microneedling Treatment?

Even though the name microneedling may sound intimidating, the collagen therapy procedure is relatively painless. Most liken the sensation to a light sunburn. Collagen therapy also one of the safest dermatology procedures because the tiny micro abrasions made in the top layer of your skin are so minute.

Sometimes a topical numbing treatment is used in combination with microneedling collagen therapy. After you meet with your team of aesthetic specialists and determine a holistic treatment plan that considers everything from nutrition to the procedure and aftercare recommendations — your appointment for collagen therapy microneedling in our office typically follows a common routine:

  1. Your aesthetic specialist washes your face.
  2. A numbing cream is applied to your face and after a couple minutes you shouldn’t feel the microneedling at all.
  3. The microneedling pen is moved across your face and focused on trouble areas.
  4. If you and your doctor have decided on a serum to be used in combination with microneedling, this is then applied.
  5. This whole procedure only lasts 15 to 20 minutes after which you’re free to return to your normal activities.

Due to the expected redness after microneedling treatments in our aesthetic center, you may opt to take the rest of the day off and rest, though it isn’t necessary.

After Your Microneedling Treatment in New York

The most common side effects of microneedling or collagen induction therapy in our center are redness, soreness and skin tightening after microneedling. You can return to your work or rest for the remainder of the day. You should stay out of the sun completely for at least a week after microneedling or collagen therapy. You can repeat microneedling treatments three to five months apart.

As with any procedure that causes perforations to the skin, there is a chance of infection, though you may use your normal beauty regimen to keep the skin clean and free of unnecessary lotions or crèmes. Your anesthetist will discuss recommendations for the best products to promote ideal healing.
